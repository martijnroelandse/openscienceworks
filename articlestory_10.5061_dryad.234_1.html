Full narrative

The Global Wood Density Database, developed by Zanne et al., represents a foundational research output in the field of ecology, particularly regarding forest biomass and carbon storage. With a total of 457 citations, its impact is evident across various scholarly publications, highlighting its integral role in furthering understanding within the discipline.

This dataset has particularly influenced the journal Forest Ecology and Management, which accounts for 39 of the citations, indicating that many researchers are leveraging this resource to support their investigations into forest ecosystems. Notably, the dataset has supported various work types, with 399 articles citing it, alongside dissertations and preprints, driving the discourse and evolution of knowledge in this area.

The dataset is extensively acknowledged across multiple countries, with the highest citation frequency from the United States (110), the United Kingdom (79), and Brazil (69). This geographical spread underscores the dataset’s international relevance and adoption in ecological research globally.

Moreover, the substantial downstream reuse of the data, quantified as 459 instances, reflects its value as a resource enabling diverse ecological studies, thus contributing to ongoing advancements in understanding wood density and its implications. This kind of active engagement emphasizes the dataset's importance as a tool for research, continuing to facilitate inquiry well beyond its initial release.
