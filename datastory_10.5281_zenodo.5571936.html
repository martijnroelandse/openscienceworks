The Story So Far

Short impact summary

The ESA WorldCover 10 m 2020 dataset, developed by a team of experts from prestigious institutions including the European Space Agency and Wageningen University & Research, has garnered significant academic attention with 512 citations and effective downstream reuse across various scientific domains. Its strong visibility is evident from 58,648 views and 160,063 downloads on the Zenodo Dataset repository, showcasing its high value and accessibility to the research community.

Full narrative

The ESA WorldCover 10 m 2020 dataset, authored by a collaborative team including Daniele Zanaga and members from key institutions such as the International Institute for Applied Systems Analysis and Brockmann Consult, represents a significant contribution to socio-environmental systems modeling. With its impressive citation count of 512, this dataset is well-integrated into the academic framework, demonstrating its relevance to ongoing research efforts.

The dataset's active community engagement is further supported by its substantial metrics on the Zenodo platform, where it has achieved 58,648 views and has been downloaded 160,063 times. These figures serve as hard evidence of the dataset's utilization and reveal a commitment to data accessibility, allowing researchers to leverage this information in various applications.

Notably, the dataset plays a crucial role in the continuing dialogue surrounding environmental issues, as indicated by its influence in top citing venues such as 'OECD regions and cities at a glance' and 'Scientific Data'. This highlights the dataset's integration into diverse research contexts, further emphasizing its scientific stewardship.

The authors have also demonstrated a strong commitment to open science practices by sharing early versions of the dataset, which has led to immediate community engagement, evidenced by two preprint citations. This proactive sharing enhances the dataset's impact and supports the ongoing evolution of research in the field.
