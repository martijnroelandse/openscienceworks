Full narrative

This preprint, authored by a collaborative team from major institutions including the University of Alberta and New York University, addresses the crucial role of FITM2 in lipid metabolism. The findings demonstrate that FITM2 deficiency leads to endoplasmic reticulum (ER) lipid accumulation and stress, which subsequently results in impaired apolipoprotein B lipidation and diminished very-low-density lipoprotein (VLDL) triglyceride secretion.
