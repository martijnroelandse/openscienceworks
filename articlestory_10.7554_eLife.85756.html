The research led by Yoshinori Aso and colleagues employs innovative methodologies to explore how learned olfactory valences influence wind-oriented movements. With a solid citation count of 37, this study has not only contributed significantly to the academic discourse but also demonstrates broad interest from the scientific community, with multiple citations appearing in high-impact venues such as bioRxiv and PLoS Computational Biology.

This reinforces the work's impact on advancing our understanding of the complex interplay between sensory processing and movement behavior in neural circuits.
