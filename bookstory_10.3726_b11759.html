Short impact summary

Published by Peter Lang Verlag in 2018, Tracey Wilen-Daugenti's Digital Disruption has found its most tangible footprint outside the formal citation record, circulating through commercial book channels and a small but notable collection of public-facing video appearances. With three recorded scholarly citations and strong commercial visibility, the book sits at the intersection of practitioner-oriented discourse and emerging academic interest in technology's social and organizational consequences.

Full narrative

The book's most prominent signal is its commercial reach. Its presence on both Amazon and Goodreads indicates that Digital Disruption was positioned and received as a trade-accessible scholarly work, reaching audiences beyond the typical institutional library circuit. These media engagements, concentrated largely around the book's 2018 release year, suggest an active public communication strategy and genuine interest from practitioners in sectors navigating technological change.
