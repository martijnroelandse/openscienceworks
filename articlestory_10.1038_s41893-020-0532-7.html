Full narrative

Published in 2020, "Hydrological limits to carbon capture and storage" by Lorenzo Rosa, Jeffrey A. Reimer, Marjorie Went, and Paolo D’Odorico represents a vital contribution to the understanding of the interplay between hydrology and carbon capture technologies. Its impact is evidenced by 114 citations across prominent venues, including Environmental Research Letters and Environmental Science & Technology. This level of citation highlights the relevance and urgency of the research topic, particularly within the context of climate change mitigation.

The study delves into how water availability and quality can significantly influence the efficiency of carbon capture and storage systems. This focus on hydrological parameters adds a crucial layer to discussions surrounding carbon management, demonstrating that the effectiveness of such technologies cannot be fully understood without considering regional water issues. This approach has resonated within the research community, with institutions from top citing countries such as China and the United States leading the conversation.

The engagement with this article is not confined to traditional academic discourse; it is part of a broader conversation around sustainability and climate resilience. Moreover, the academic discourse surrounding the research is critical for advancing our understanding of both hydrology and carbon capture strategies. As evidenced by the substantial number of citations, the work has proven instrumental in enabling downstream research, showcasing the authors' contributions to fostering a deeper comprehension of these interconnected fields.
