Full narrative

The Ryerson Audio-Visual Database of Emotional Speech and Song (RAVDESS), published in 2018 and hosted on the Zenodo platform, represents a foundational dataset that has captured substantial interest within the academic community. The dataset has garnered 329 citations, reflecting its pivotal role in advancing research in the realm of emotional expression and affective computing.

The dataset's utility is further evidenced by the impressive metrics from DataCite, which indicate that it has been viewed 345,173 times and downloaded over 1.2 million times. These numbers serve as hard evidence of its value to researchers, demonstrating active community reuse and strong engagement with its technical content. The dataset's influence spans various types of academic work, with the majority being articles (289), supplemented by preprints and book chapters.

Key institutions such as the University of Wisconsin–River Falls and Toronto Metropolitan University lend credibility to the dataset, indicating a solid institutional backing that enriches its academic reputation. The geographic footprint of the research is also notable, with significant citations emerging from countries like China, India, and the United States, among others. This international interest underscores the global relevance of emotional speech data in various research contexts.

Moreover, the dataset's early sharing practices have been commendable, evidenced by preprint citations totaling 6. This indicates that the authors have engaged with the community long before formal publication, fostering early adoption and discussion within their field. In summary, the RAVDESS dataset not only contributes to the growing body of research in emotional speech and song but also exemplifies the principles of open science through its extensive reuse and dissemination.
