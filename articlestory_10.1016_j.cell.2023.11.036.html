Short impact summary

Full narrative

Investigating the implications of human inherited CCR2 deficiency, the research published in Cell by Anna‐Lena Neehus and colleagues advances knowledge on progressive polycystic lung disease. This work not only emphasizes the genetic underpinnings of the disease but also underscores the vital role of CCR2 in pulmonary health, further contextualized by the study's acknowledgement from 51 citations across various scholarly avenues.

The strong institutional backing for this research, highlighted by affiliations with leading medical institutions such as Cincinnati Children's Hospital Medical Center and Tehran University of Medical Sciences, underscores the collaborative effort in tackling complex health challenges. Moreover, the inclusion of 73 Research Resource Identifiers (RRIDs) reflects a commitment to technical reproducibility, which is essential for advancing research reliability and fostering collaborative science.
