The Story So Far

Short impact summary

Published in 2020 by Peter Lang Verlag, Les défis de la diversité culturelle dans le monde du travail au XXIe siècle by Ariane Le Moing, Saïd Ouaked, and Christèle Le Bihan is a scholarly contribution to the ongoing European debate on cultural diversity in the workplace. The book has achieved commercial distribution presence and carries thematic alignment with the UN Sustainable Development Goal on reduced inequalities, though its formal citation footprint remains nascent at this stage.

Full narrative

The book appears as a volume in Peter Lang's long-running Travail et Société / Work and Society series, a bilingual imprint that has, over several decades, hosted rigorous scholarship on European labour markets, social policy, and employment practice. Peer volumes in the same series have addressed topics ranging from trade union dynamics in the European Union to diversity policies and professional career support, situating this work within a well-established intellectual community preoccupied with equity and inclusion in working life. The series context lends the volume a recognised scholarly platform, even as its citation record is still developing.

At its thematic core, the book engages directly with questions of cultural pluralism and workplace integration — challenges that have grown in urgency across twenty-first-century labour markets shaped by migration, globalisation, and shifting demographic compositions. Two of the authors, Saïd Ouaked and Christèle Le Bihan, are affiliated with Les Afriques dans le Monde, a research unit whose expertise in African diasporas and transnational identities lends the volume a distinctive empirical and geopolitical perspective that distinguishes it from purely Eurocentric treatments of workplace diversity.
