The Story So Far

Short impact summary

The research on proteolysis-targeting chimeras (PROTACs) with reduced off-target effects has garnered significant academic attention, evidenced by 123 citations since its release in Nature Chemistry. The authors, including researchers from the Broad Institute and Harvard University, have contributed to the early sharing of this work, leading to 14 preprint citations and generating robust discussions within the scientific community.

Full narrative

The publication of this research on proteolysis-targeting chimeras showcases a substantial academic impact, as indicated by its 123 citations in various scientific venues, prominently including the Journal of Medicinal Chemistry. This reflects not only the relevance of the findings within the discipline but also suggests an active engagement from the broader research community.

The authors have adopted a commendable approach to open science by making an early version of their findings accessible as a preprint. This practice has resulted in significant community interactions, demonstrated by 14 preprint citations and 5 events surrounding the preprint. Such early dissemination has allowed the work to influence ongoing discussions and research themes in the field.

Moreover, the research has sparked debate on platforms like Reddit, accentuating its societal relevance and the interest it has generated beyond traditional academic circles. This aspect highlights the interplay between scientific inquiry and public discourse, enriching the understanding and applicability of these novel chimeras.

The financial backing from prominent agencies such as the NIH and the Department of Defense underscores the institutional investment in this research area, validating the significance of developing PROTACs with fewer off-target effects. The resulting findings could potentially pave the way for innovative therapeutic strategies in drug development, addressing critical barriers currently faced in the field.
