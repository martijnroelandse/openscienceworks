Full narrative

The software tool PyPSA (Python for Power System Analysis), authored by Tom Brown, Jonas Hörsch, and David Schlachtberger, has made a notable impact in the field of power system analysis since its introduction in 2026. With a robust citation count of 528, this tool has been widely recognized and utilized by researchers, indicating its relevance and importance within the academic community.

PyPSA's development is anchored by the esteemed Frankfurt Institute for Advanced Studies, highlighting the institutional backing that lends credibility to the work. The tool's significant uptake is reflected not only in its citations but also in its quantitative engagement metrics, with 5,318 views and 981 downloads documented through DataCite. These figures are a testament to the software's technical value and its widespread applicability among researchers and practitioners in the field.

In terms of its geographical influence, the software has drawn particular attention in Germany, which accounts for 179 of the total citations, underscoring its impact in this key academic hub. Other countries that have recognized its value include the United States, the United Kingdom, Denmark, and Italy, showcasing a diverse and international interest in the tool. This cross-border engagement further solidifies PyPSA's role as a fundamental resource in power system analysis.

The commitment to making this software tool accessible and useful is evident in its usage metrics. The active community reuse, exemplified by the number of views and downloads, aligns with emerging trends in open science and the sharing of research outputs. By facilitating widespread accessibility, PyPSA not only supports ongoing research and innovation but also fosters collaborative efforts in the field of power system analysis.
