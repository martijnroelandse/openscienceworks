The Story So Far

Short impact summary

Despite its publication in 2014, "Opening Science" has yet to garner citations or community discourse, suggesting limited immediate impact or engagement within academic circles or public platforms.

Full narrative

"Opening Science," published by Springer Nature in 2014, presents perspectives and insights into the evolving landscape of scientific research and dissemination. However, the book currently stands at zero citations, indicating that it has not yet been recognized or referenced within subsequent academic literature. This lack of citations could suggest that the arguments and ideas presented have either not resonated within the research community or that they may require further dissemination to achieve wider recognition.

Furthermore, the absence of metrics under "High-Fidelity Social & Community Discourse" points to a similar trend in public engagement. There are no mentions of the book across prominent platforms such as Wikipedia, Reddit, or Bluesky, suggesting limited public or societal interest in the themes it explores. This lack of community discourse might be indicative of the challenges in breaking through the noise of contemporary academic outputs and achieving a footprint in both academic and public conversations.

Additionally, there have been no recorded events or engagements linked to "Opening Science," further underlining that the text has not catalyzed any discussions or gatherings around its subject matter. The book's potential to serve as a reference point for synthesis is hinted at by its strong inferred roles, particularly as a commercial linkage via platforms like Amazon and Goodreads. Yet, the actual scholarly impact remains negligible without evidence of academic engagement.

Ultimately, while "Opening Science" presents significant themes relevant to open science practices, its lack of citations, community interaction, and apparent visibility suggest that it has yet to achieve its intended impact. The path forward for this scholarly work may lie in reinvigorating outreach strategies to connect with relevant audiences, both within academia and the broader public sphere, to enhance its influence and recognition in the field.
