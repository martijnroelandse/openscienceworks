Full narrative

The "IoT-23" dataset, developed by researchers Sebastián García, Agustin Parmisano, and María José Erquiaga from the Czech Technical University in Prague, provides critical insights into the behavior of both malicious and benign Internet of Things (IoT) network traffic. Since its release in 2020, the dataset has garnered considerable academic attention, evident in its 228 citations, reflecting its utility in advancing cybersecurity research, particularly regarding IoT devices.

Notably, the dataset has been extensively reused in subsequent studies, with a proven downstream reuse metric of 231. This high level of reuse underscores its role as a foundational resource that supports ongoing research in identifying and addressing security vulnerabilities within IoT environments. The dataset's accessibility on Zenodo further enhances its reach, allowing researchers and practitioners easy access to high-quality data.

The academic impact of "IoT-23" is further illustrated by its broad citation base, with notable referencing in leading venues such as IEEE Access and Computer Networks. The dataset serves as a foundational tool aiding scholars from various countries, including the United States, India, and China, in conducting cutting-edge research into IoT security challenges.

The dataset's open access nature, while not explicitly stated, suggests a commitment to transparency and data sharing, which aligns with best practices in research stewardship. As the landscape of IoT technology continues to evolve, the insights derived from the "IoT-23" dataset are likely to play a crucial role in the fields of cybersecurity and technology risk management, fostering improved methodologies for securing IoT networks.
