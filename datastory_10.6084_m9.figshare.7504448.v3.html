Full narrative

The Global Aridity Index and Potential Evapotranspiration (ET0) Climate Database v2, hosted on Figshare, has emerged as a significant dataset in the field of climate science since its release in 2019. With a total of 5,116 views and 1,195 downloads, it demonstrates active community engagement and showcases its utility as a valuable resource for researchers focusing on climate variability and water resource management.

Citations play a crucial role in assessing the impact of scholarly outputs, and this dataset has been cited 336 times. Notably, the top citing venues include influential platforms such as bioRxiv, Global Change Biology, and Nature Communications, illustrating its relevance across a spectrum of research contexts. Furthermore, the dataset has facilitated proven downstream reuse, as indicated by its reuse citation count of 359, revealing its widespread application in various studies.

The geographic reach of the dataset is noteworthy, with top citing countries including the United States, Germany, and China. This spread indicates the dataset's global significance and the collaborative nature of climate research across different institutions and regions. Such international engagement underscores the dataset's role in fostering a collaborative environment for scientific inquiry.

Overall, the Global Aridity Index and Potential Evapotranspiration (ET0) Climate Database v2 stands as a testament to the importance of open data in scientific research. Its availability contributes to enhanced understanding and innovative studies in the climate arena, as evidenced by both its citation metrics and widespread utilization in active research efforts globally.
